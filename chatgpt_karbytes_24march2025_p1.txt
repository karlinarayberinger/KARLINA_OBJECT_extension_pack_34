SPDC is a quantum optical process where:

- Inside the crystal, one photon splits into two lower-energy photons.

- These two photons are:

+ Created simultaneously.

+ Quantum entangled in one or more degrees of freedom (like polarization, momentum, energy, or position).

- Idler photon

üß™ How It‚Äôs Done in the Lab

1. Photon Source (Pump Laser)

- A UV laser (often around 405 nm) fires photons into the crystal.

- These photons are much higher energy than the resulting photon pairs.

2. Nonlinear Crystal (e.g., BBO)

- This crystal has special optical properties that allow for down-conversion.

- One pump photon is converted into two entangled infrared photons (often around 810 nm each).

- There are two types of SPDC setups:

+ Type I: Both output photons have the same polarization.

+ Type II: Output photons have orthogonal polarizations (e.g., one horizontal, one vertical).

- The crystal is aligned so that the emitted photon pairs exit at specific angles‚Äîusually forming two cones of light that intersect.

- Entangled pairs are collected at points where the cones overlap.

üîπ Photons Have Zero Rest Mass

- Photons are massless particles in terms of rest mass.

- Their energy is related to their frequency by this equation:

Where:

E is energy,

h is Planck‚Äôs constant,

f is frequency.

- A high-energy pump photon (e.g., 405 nm) is absorbed by a nonlinear crystal.

- It ‚Äúsplits‚Äù into two lower-energy photons (e.g., each 810 nm).

‚úÖ Energy Conservation:

E_pump = E_signal + E_idler

Each daughter photon has less energy, hence lower frequency (longer wavelength).

üî∏ Momentum Is Also Conserved:

p = E / c = (h * f) / c = h / Œª

So momentum conservation also applies:

p_pump = p_signal + p_idler

That‚Äôs why the photons emerge at complementary angles forming conical emission patterns from the crystal.

üß¨ Mass ‚â† Energy in This Case

E = m * (c ^ 2)

- Instead, photon energy is fully expressed as radiative (kinetic) energy, not mass.

üß† Bottom Line:

- Original photon: mass = 0, energy = E = h * f

- Two new photons: each mass = 0, energy = h * f_1 and h * f_2

- Total energy and momentum are conserved

- No change in rest mass occurs, because there was no rest mass to begin with.
